Paul Kinlan
+Tantek Çelik +Michael Hausenblas Facebook appear to have a completely different intent [argh - sorry no pun intended] to what we know as actions and intents. The FB approach is about telling Facebook what you are doing and what you are doing it on, which in turn is about connecting your actions with the people around you. The first line of the developer documentation states "Action specs can be used in ad targeting to reach out to people based on their actions." [1] - this to me a crazy strong move against the sem-web, that adds people and what they do in to the graph.

If you have a verb/action like "later" or "save" or "edit" and "pick" in the FB world then these don't appear (in my understanding) to fit in to a model that would work and allow you to actually perform actions on the data.

Do these actions let you edit an image or save a document to your Instapaper? No. It is a different thing, it is sharing your actions on objects to Facebook and your friends.

Reading the mechanics of the spec this doesn't appear to be the case either, and from a publishers perspective there is actually quite a lot of work that you need to do, both on the client and server.

Will this be popular with publishers and developers? Yes if it lets them make more money. Are they the same as Web Actions and Web Intents? No - not by a long shot.

[1] https://developers.facebook.com/docs/reference/ads-api/action-specs-custom/
[2] https://developers.facebook.com/docs/beta/opengraph/#actions-objects.﻿
